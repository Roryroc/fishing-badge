Click each hazard card to reveal what it is and how to prevent or respond to it. Learn all 6 to be prepared!

⚡
Lightning
Get off the water immediately. Seek shelter away from tall trees. Wait 30 min after last thunder.
🌊
Falling In
Always wear a PFD near water. Tell someone your location. Never fish alone near fast water.
🎣
Hooks & Lures
Never cast near people. Keep hooks capped when not in use. Use barbless hooks when possible.
☀️
Sun/Heat
Wear sunscreen, hat, and UV clothing. Drink water every 20 min. Recognize heat exhaustion signs.
🐍
Wildlife
Watch where you step in tall grass. Identify venomous snakes in your area. Don't handle unknown fish.
🥶
Cold Water
Cold water shock can incapacitate in seconds. Wear a PFD, dress in layers, and never ice fish alone.

🪝 Artificial Lures:

🐟
Crankbait
Dives & wiggles
Crankbait: Hard plastic lure with a lip that makes it dive and wobble. Cast and reel steadily. Best for bass and walleye near structure.
✨
Spinner
Blade lure
Spinner: Metal blade spins in water creating flash and vibration. Reel at steady speed. Great for trout, bass, and pike.
🦗
Soft Plastic Worm
Rubber worm
Soft Plastic Worm: Rubber worm on a hook. Drag slowly on the bottom. Extremely effective for bass year-round.
🪁
Spoon
Metal spoon
Spoon: Curved metal lure that wobbles and flashes like a wounded baitfish. Cast and retrieve at varying speeds for trout, salmon, and bass.
🦟
Jig
Weighted head
Jig: Weighted hook with a skirt or soft body. Bounce along the bottom with a hop-and-fall motion. One of the most versatile lures for all game fish.

🪱 Natural Baits:

🪱
Nightcrawler
Live worm
Nightcrawler: The classic fishing worm. Thread onto hook and let sink. Works on almost any freshwater fish — panfish, bass, catfish, trout.
🦗
Cricket/Grasshopper
Live insect
Cricket: Excellent live bait for panfish and trout. Hook through the collar. Works great under a bobber near surface.
🦐
Minnow
Live baitfish
Minnow: Hook through the lips or back. Let it swim naturally. Deadly for bass, walleye, perch, and pike. Do not release baitfish!
🦞
Crayfish
Live crustacean
Crayfish: Hook through the tail. Crawl slowly along rocky bottom. Bass and smallmouth can't resist these!
🐛
Salmon Eggs
Egg cluster
Salmon Eggs: Single egg or cluster on a small hook. Float downstream in rivers. Top bait for trout and steelhead.

Leave No Trace & Catch and Release
✅ ▼
Explain the LNT Seven Principles and Outdoor Code as they relate to fishing. Discuss catch-and-release and describe how to safely release a fish.
📚 Resources
🌐 7 LNT Principles ▶ LNT Basics ▶ Safe Catch & Release ▶ Ethical C&R Fishing
Part A — Leave No Trace
Explain the importance of the LNT Seven Principles and how they positively impact fishing resources.
🎯 Interactive Activity
LNT Principles — Tap to Reveal How They Apply to Fishing
📍 1. Plan Ahead & Prepare
Know regulations before you go. Check if licenses, size limits, or seasonal closures apply. Bring proper gear to avoid waste or damage.
🌿 2. Travel on Durable Surfaces
Stick to established banks, docks, and paths. Avoid trampling vegetation on riverbanks that fish and other wildlife depend on.
🗑️ 3. Dispose of Waste Properly
Pack out all trash including fishing line, bait containers, and food scraps. Never leave monofilament — it entangles wildlife.
🌸 4. Leave What You Find
Don't disturb rocks, logs, or underwater habitat. These are spawning and shelter areas for fish and other aquatic life.
🔥 5. Minimize Campfire Impacts
If you cook your catch streamside, use established fire rings. Fully extinguish fires and don't leave ash or debris near the water.
🦅 6. Respect Wildlife
Handle fish gently and quickly. Don't disturb nesting birds near the water. Don't leave bait scraps that attract predators.
🤝 7. Be Considerate of Others
Give other anglers space. Keep noise down. Don't crowd other fishers at a productive spot. Leave it better than you found it.
